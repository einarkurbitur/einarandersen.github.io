Pirate Jet

A flying podcast that will take over your browser at any moment. I designed a chrome extension that overlays a website over your current website and “hijacks” your browser. While you are hijacked the only thing you can do is listen to the podcast. After few minutes the podcast flies away, but it can come back at any moment. You can download the extension here

The Space Program

A series of posters created for a fictional Sci-Fi festival in the Netherlands called "The Space Program". I created a fictional galaxy and for each location of the festival I created a planet that had it’s own mass, rotation speed and moon. The moon indicates the date when the festival will be on that "planet". Each poster is a unique snapshot of the rotation of the galaxy.

The Modern Decay

A researched into the digital decay of images, more specifically memes. As memes get popular, the get screenshot, re-uploaded, filtered and screenshot again. With every screenshot the image gets compressed a bit more, further decaying the meme. You can see the quality of the meme in the state of it. The danker the meme, the shittier the quality. From that I created a digital wallpaper/screensaver with my desktop and memes that slowly decays. The decay creates a nice pattern of pixels and colors which I used to print out a full size wallpaper.

Untitled (Home)

A picture of a footstep on the moon reminds of my youth when I went out in the morning and stepped in freshly fallen snow. I realized the moon and Iceland were eerily similar in a strange and calming way. Suddenly the moon felt like home. The only thing inbetween me and my home is the mat.

Mobius Limbs

A project where I designed posters for a fictional band. The band, called Mobius limbs, is a band that mixes Goa Trance and New Wave. The band promote an anti capitalistic message with a spiritual twist. Most of the concerts are a spontaneous event filled with out of this world experience.
The posters are silk printed in A2 on existing posters.

The Complete Encyclopedia of Unfinished Art

After browsing the Metropolitian Museum on archive.org I noticed that there were images that had not uploaded properly or were corrupt in any other way. I collected and catagorized them and from there created an encyclopedia of unfinished art.

Cycle 13999

A story of a woman stuck in a dystopian world ruled by computers, where days no longer exist. Only cycles. Cycles of work. One cycle she sees a flash of text saying “You will be the queen of the stars”. It sparks a thought in her head. She tries to get out but as she tries to fight the computers in her mind she goes insane.
